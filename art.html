Mars

Luke Jerram, a British artist, recently completed the piece Mars. The sculpture, which features NASA images of the Martian terrain, is enormous. Viewers are given a bird's-eye view of Mars through the artwork, as if we were satellites mapping and closely inspecting the planet's surface. The artwork brings us to this arid wasteland to picture what it could be like to set foot on this amazing planet. Every day from noon to 8:00 p.m., Mars will be open.

Les Girafes

One of the most well-known street theatre troupes in Europe, Compagnie Off, will perform in Galway with a herd of red giraffes. The giraffes will saunter through Galway's streets while being led by a beloved opera diva. A group of circus ringmasters accompany the show, which heightens the intensity and vitality of this enchanted street procession. Starting at Eyre Square, the parade will finish at Spanish Arch.

A Handful of Dreams

The outdoor performance "A Handful of Dreams" by a brilliant group of five aerial dancers and two live musicians doesn't require reservations. The specifically created outdoor setup gives the artists the ideal stage to display their talents, and the show is sure to touch everyone in the audience with its happy and uplifting music.

Miracoco Luminarium

These interactive walk-in sculptures, created by Alan Parkinson, have been displayed all around the world, from the Sydney Opera House in Australia to the Guggenheim in Spain. All ages can experience awe and enchantment at the luminarium, a beautiful maze of meandering passageways, soaring domes, and sparkling light. Come and be fascinated by this fascinating universe.
