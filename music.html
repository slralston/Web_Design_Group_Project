2023 Music Shows

Fat Freddy’s Drop

Fat Freddy's Drop, the famous seven-piece band from New Zealand, known for their love of analog techno, house, soul, dub, reggae, disco, rhythm and blues, is scheduled to play at the Heineken Big Top. They have a massive fan following in New Zealand and Australia due to their energetic live performances. The Guardian has described their live gig as a must-see performance and given it a five-star rating. Fat Freddy's Drop will take the stage on Thursday 20 July.

Kaiser Chiefs

Kaiser Chiefs, one of the UK's most successful bands, have been entertaining audiences for almost two decades with their chart-topping hits and exceptional live shows. The band has received several accolades, including three Brit Awards, an NME award for Best Album, and a nomination for the Mercury Prize. They have also shared the stage with music legends such as U2, Green Day, and Foo Fighters. In the closing ceremony of the 2012 London Olympics, they performed "Pinball Wizard" by The Who, leaving a lasting impression on the audience.

Bell X1

Bell X1, one of the most popular bands from Ireland, is returning to the Galway International Arts Festival, and their performance at the Heineken Big Top on Friday 28 July is expected to be one of the highlights of the summer. Known for their musical versatility and experimentation, Bell X1's albums incorporate a wide range of genres, including alternative, indie, hard rock, post-punk, acoustic, lo-fi, folk-rock, ballad, pop, synth-pop, new wave, and electronica.

DruidO’Casey

Druid, a theatre company, is presenting a theatrical event, DruidO'Casey, based on the works of O'Casey, one of the greatest Irish stories ever told. The event will weave O'Casey's three plays into an epic production of conflict, national identity, and the human toll of war, marking the centenary of Ireland's rebirth in the fires of rebellion and war. The production, directed by Tony Award winner Garry Hynes, will feature a cast of 18 actors performing all three plays in one day, drawing parallels between Ireland's past and the current international scenario.
